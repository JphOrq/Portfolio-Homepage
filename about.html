About Me

I am a results-driven professional who began my career in the construction industry as a Purchaser/Procurement Officer, where I developed strong competencies in supplier coordination, canvassing, inventory management, and cost control. Through this experience, I built a solid foundation in analytical thinking, process optimization, and data management.

Driven by a strong interest in technology, I successfully transitioned into the IT field and gained hands-on experience as a Developer/Programmer. In my current role, I provide technical support in the development, implementation, and maintenance of web-based systems for the National Tuberculosis (TB) Program. I am actively involved in managing and enhancing the Integrated Tuberculosis Information System (ITIS), ensuring data accuracy, system reliability, and compliance with government standards.

My responsibilities include developing and maintaining system features using PHP, MySQL(MariaDB), and CodeIgniter within a XAMPP environment. I also handle database management tasks such as data validation, report generation, and regular system backups to ensure data integrity and availability. In addition, I administer and maintain the National Tuberculosis Control Program website using WordPress, ensuring timely updates, accessibility, and optimal performance.

I also serve as an administrator of web application-related accounts, where I extract, analyze, and generate critical data reports required by the program. This role has strengthened my ability to bridge technical development with real-world operational and reporting needs.

I have obtained a Certificate of Competency in Web Development NC III , and continue to expand my technical expertise in ASP.NET MVC, .NET Core, and Microsoft SQL Server. With a combination of procurement experience and IT skills, I bring a unique perspective in developing efficient, data-driven solutions.

I am committed to continuous learning and professional growth, with the goal of contributing meaningfully to system development, process improvement, and digital transformation initiatives.
